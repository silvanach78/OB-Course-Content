Q. I read the instructions for the consulting project, but I am still not sure about what news event would be appropriate for the project

A. To determine if a news event is suitable for your consulting project, I encourage you to consider the following:

  • Current and Business-Related. The event should be happening in the present (or very recently) and must involve a business organization. Avoid historical scandals like Enron or Theranos, as these are not current.
  • Ethics-Related Issue. The news event must involve an ethical or social responsibility dilemma within the organization. This could be related to discrimination, corporate social responsibility failures, regulatory violations, environmental harm, or other issues that raise ethical/moral questions for the business.
  • Actionable for a Decision-Maker. The problem must be something that a manager or decision-maker in the company can address and take control over. For example, if the issue is related to how the company treats its employees or customers, the decision-maker could be the CEO or Director of Human Resources. If it’s a strategic shift, you would advise the CEO.

If you come across a news event and are unsure whether it fits, you may want to ask yourself:

  • Does this issue involve ethical decision-making or a moral dilemma?
  • Is there a clear business entity and decision-maker involved?
  • Can the problem be addressed using the ethical frameworks we've studied?

If you answered “yes” to these questions, the event is likely appropriate for your consulting project. If you're uncertain, feel free to run the example by me for feedback.

Q. I have been thinking of what alternatives I should include. I often find that there are multiple ways to approach a problem. Is there a "right" answer, and how should I approach this situation?

A. Great observation! There are often multiple ways to solve a problem, especially in the context of organizational behavior. In real-world business scenarios, there is rarely one "correct" answer or solution. In fact, more often than not there are many viable approaches, each with its own set of advantages and trade-offs. For our consulting project, the goal is to provide well-reasoned and feasible solutions based on the ethical frameworks and stakeholder impacts we’ve studied. This is how you can approach this situation:

  • Present multiple (at least three), viable alternatives, each with its own advantages, disadvantages, and ethical implications. Make sure the alternatives are practical and consider the stakeholders and ethical frameworks.
  • Evaluate each alternative weighing the pros and cons of each option. Consider:
    • Stakeholder Impact. How will each alternative affect different stakeholders?
    • Ethical Implications. Use ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology, light of day, cultural relativism, legalism) to analyze each option’s moral soundness.
    • Feasibility. Is the alternative realistic for the decision-maker to implement?

Your final recommendation doesn’t need to be “perfect,” but it should reflect thoughtful consideration of the alternatives. The goal is to propose a solution that aligns with the company’s values, and aligns with the ethical framework you selected for your analysis.

Ultimately, what’s most important is not finding the “right” answer, but demonstrating that you have critically analyzed the problem from different perspectives and are providing a well-supported recommendation. There’s often more than one acceptable solution as long as it is backed by strong reasoning.

Q. What should I consider when writing the pros and cons of each alternative course of action?

A. When thinking about the advantages of each alternative in your consulting project, it’s crucial to remember that an advantage should directly benefit one or more stakeholders. To ensure that you are providing thoughtful and relevant alternatives, ask yourself the following questions:

  • What stakeholders benefit from this alternative?
    Identify which individuals or groups (e.g., employees, customers, shareholders, the community, etc.) will be positively impacted if the organization implements this option.
  • In what way do they benefit?
    Consider the specific type of benefit. For example:
    • Does the alternative improve working conditions for employees?
    • Will it enhance customer satisfaction or loyalty?
    • Could it lead to long-term financial gains for shareholders?
    • Does it have a positive impact on the environment or society at large?

By focusing on how different stakeholders benefit from each alternative, you’ll ensure that your analysis is comprehensive and aligned with the ethical frameworks we’ve studied. Always make sure that each advantage/disadvantage ties back to a specific benefit/harm for one or more key stakeholders.

Q. I read in the syllabus that we are allowed to use AI to help with assignments. Does that mean I can use ChatGPT or Copilot?

A. Yes, you are allowed to use ChatGPT, Copilot or any AI of your choice. Not being familiar with those tools will put you at a disadvantage in school and at work. I expect that most students are already very familiar with AI tools and use them.

To ensure that those tools are used appropriately, you must follow the guidelines described in the syllabus, which I am copying below:

Permitted Uses of AI

  • Idea Generation: Students may use AI to help brainstorm ideas for assignments and projects.
  • Getting Started: AI may be used to draft initial outlines or concepts to help you get started on your assignments.
  • Learning Structure: Students may use AI to get ideas for the formatting or structure of a document, and to organize their thoughts more effectively.
  • Document Editing: Students can use AI to assist with editing their documents by correcting spelling and grammar, suggesting improved paragraph and paper structures, and even rewriting paragraphs to make them more concise. This can be a useful exercise to help improve writing skills. However, be aware that AI tools often produce verbose text. Additionally, if you use AI for rewriting paragraphs, your paper might be flagged for review by TurnItIn. I expect that your assignment will show 0% "AI writing detected". Additionally, you must include a link or copy of the chat/interaction with the AI in your submission.

Prohibited Uses of AI

  • Copying AI-Generated Content: Students must not copy and paste AI-generated content and present it as their own work. All AI-generated content must be significantly modified and integrated into your own writing.
  • Plagiarism: Failure to credit the use of AI tools will be considered plagiarism, equivalent to using non-credited content from any other source. Plagiarism will be handled according to UHCL's academic honesty policy.

Student Responsibilities When Using AI

  • Crediting AI Use. Any use of AI in your assignments must be properly credited. This includes citing in within the text or your paper and in your references list.
  • Citing AI: Clearly cite the AI tool used in in-text citations (e.g., (OpenAI, 2024)), and in the list of references.
    • e.g., OpenAI. (2024). ChatGPT (June 29 version) [Large language model]. Prompt: "Share what you know about company's XYZ safety failures." Response received on June 25, 2024.https://chat.openai.com/chat
  • Including Prompts and Responses: You will share the prompt you used with the AI tool and the corresponding generated response in the link you include in your reference.
  • Students should be aware of the limitations of even the best AI tools. Ultimately, it is you, the student, who is responsible for all the content submitted in their assignments.
  • Fact-Checking:Students are responsible for verifying the accuracy of any information generated by AI tools. AI sometimes generates incorrect information that is very believable and plausible sounding. When interacting with AI, if you are trying to obtain important information, it is your responsibility to fact-check with a high-quality source to ensure that all facts and data used in your assignments are accurate and reliable.

Policy Enforcement:

Any assignment found to contain AI-generated content that is not properly credited or is passed off as the student's own work will be subject to the same penalties as other forms of plagiarism, including receiving a zero for the assignment and potential referral to the dean of students for further disciplinary action.

By adhering to these guidelines, all students can benefit from the use of AI tools while maintaining academic integrity and producing high-quality, original work. If you have any questions about the appropriate use of AI in your assignments, please do not hesitate to ask for clarification.
